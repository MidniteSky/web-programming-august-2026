1. Semantic HTML & real buttons

Avoid

<div onclick="...">Delete</div>

Delete (a div - can't Tab to it)
Prefer

<button>Delete</button>

2. Label every input

Avoid

Placeholder only - no label.

Prefer

Icon-only buttons need an aria-label (see demo 08): a bare bin icon reads as just "button" otherwise.

3. Enough contrast to read

Avoid

Light grey text on white. Can you read this comfortably? Many can't.

Prefer

Dark text on white - clear for everyone. Aim for a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for normal text.

4. Keyboard & a visible focus ring

Put your mouse down. Press Tab repeatedly and watch the outline move between the links, inputs and buttons - then Enter or Space to activate. Everything must be reachable this way, and you must be able to SEE where you are.

Never hide the focus ring with outline: none; and nothing to replace it - it strands keyboard users. Bootstrap gives focused controls a clear ring by default; your Day 2 CSS added one on inputs too.

The good news: you already do most of this. Semantic HTML has been the house rule since Day 1, your forms have had labels all week, and your images have carried alt text. Accessibility is largely a by-product of building things properly - which is exactly why we insisted on it from the start.
